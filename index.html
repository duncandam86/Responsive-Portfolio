About Me


Unlike most people, I have an unusual and rather mobile childhood. I was born in the USSR in the 80s but grew up Vietnam. At 8 years old, I moved to Perth, a city in the westcoast of Australia, with my family. For 6 years, my life was full of constant relocation to different countries around the South Pacific due to my dad's job. In 2000, I received a scholarship to attend a boarding school in London, I packed my bag and moved to the UK. I spent 5 years in London. And on a random night in my senior year, one of my closest friends suggested her desire to move to America. Instantly, I knew a new adventure was calling my name. A year later, I packed my bag one more time and moved across the Atlantic to America. I landed in Crawfordsville, IN - the smallest town that I have ever lived until this day - to attend college. I finally moved to Chicago in the Fall of 2009 to start my PhD at Northwestern. Since then I have been at the institution as a student, fellow and now a research staff.

As a career scientist, my research at Northwestern focused on developing new therapeutic strategy for diseases. We employed a new found technology which used materials at nanoscale level to deliver active drugs to the disease sites. Our technology have worked in various diseases ranging from cancer to wound healing. My current endeavor is to apply this technology in immune diseases, especially ones trigger by viral infection. You can see more of my recent research at Google Scholar .

I enjoy traveling, working out and living a healthy lifestyle. I've spent a lot of my time volunteering especially with LGBT organization. I'm a member of the Chicago Gay Men's Chorus, the outNETWORK at Feinberg School of Medicine, Chicago Pride Parade Organization, The National Queer Asian Pacific Islander Alliance.
